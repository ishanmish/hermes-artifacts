Bogotá/Medellín -> Leticia -> Iquitos by fast boat: practical briefing

Last checked: 2026-05-23 12:33 IST

Bottom-line recommendation

If the goal is to reach Iquitos with the least friction, start from Bogotá, fly to Leticia, spend at least one night there, then cross to Santa Rosa, Peru and take the fast boat/ferry to Iquitos. Medellín is possible, but usually adds a connection via Bogotá and more schedule risk.

For the boat, the safest identifiable primary option is CONFLUAM / Consorcio Fluvial del Amazonas / Ferry Amazonas. It has an official site, published fares and schedule claims, and a larger ferry-style vessel. Transporte Fluvial Zoe Alexa also appears in recent traveler reports and may be cheaper, but it is more of a locally-confirmed/WhatsApp/on-the-ground option rather than a polished online-booking product.

Do not plan this as a tight same-day flight-to-boat connection. Treat Leticia/Santa Rosa as a border/logistics day. The main thing that goes wrong is not the river itself; it is missing schedules, unclear ticketing, or immigration stamps.

Route overview

  1. Fly to Leticia, Colombia.
  • Bogotá -> Leticia: normally the cleanest, around 2 hours nonstop when available.
  • Medellín -> Leticia: usually via Bogotá; allow 4-7+ hours depending on connection.
  • There is no sensible overland route from interior Colombia to Leticia for a normal traveler.
  1. In Leticia, handle Colombia exit formalities.
  • Travelers commonly report going to the airport/migration office for the Colombia exit stamp.
  • Complete any Colombia Check-Mig/pre-registration requirements if applicable.
  1. Cross from Leticia to Santa Rosa, Peru.
  • Fast boats to Iquitos generally leave from Santa Rosa, not Leticia itself.
  • Leticia, Tabatinga (Brazil), and Santa Rosa (Peru) are effectively a tri-border cluster. Local movement can feel informal, but international onward travel is not informal: get the stamps right.
  1. Enter Peru at Santa Rosa.
  • Do the Peru entry stamp/control before continuing to Iquitos.
  • This is the step to be deliberate about; do not rely on “everyone crosses casually” as proof you are legally entered.
  1. Take the fast boat/ferry from Santa Rosa to Iquitos.
  • CONFLUAM official site currently describes Iquitos <-> Santa Rosa service, around 12-15 hours, larger ferry/catamaran-style boats, and frontier-to-Iquitos departures on Tue/Thu/Sat.
  • Traveler reports vary widely on actual time. Plan around 12-20+ hours, with some accounts in the 15-23 hour range depending on current, stops, operator, and direction.

Boat operators

1. CONFLUAM / Consorcio Fluvial del Amazonas / Ferry Amazonas

Best default choice if schedule and seats work.

Why:

  • Official web presence: https://confluam.pe/
  • Official-site claims include Ferry Amazonas I/II, larger catamaran-style vessels, around 300-350 passenger capacity, air conditioning, panoramic windows, onboard café/snacks, and luggage policy.
  • Published fare on the site: Santa Rosa -> Iquitos normal S/250, foreign passenger S/300.
  • Published baggage allowance: maximum 2 bags up to 30 kg; excess charged per kg.
  • Published schedule narrative: Iquitos departures Monday/Wednesday/Friday and frontier/Santa Rosa departures Tuesday/Thursday/Saturday.

Caveats:

  • The site is useful, but still verify by WhatsApp/phone or locally in Leticia/Santa Rosa before locking your trip.
  • Online ferry information in this region goes stale often.
  • A “modern ferry” does not mean a luxury cruise; it is still long public transport on the Amazon.

2. Transporte Fluvial Zoe Alexa

Possible budget/local alternative; use only after local confirmation.

Why consider it:

  • Appears in recent traveler/agency/Reddit-style reports as an operating fast-boat option.
  • Reports suggest it can be cheaper than the official foreigner CONFLUAM fare.

Caveats:

  • Less reliable online footprint.
  • You may need WhatsApp/local ticket purchase.
  • Confirm exact departure point, time, fare, luggage rules, and whether it is actually running on your date.

What the boat is like

Expect public river transport, not a scenic tourist cruise. For CONFLUAM/Ferry Amazonas, the official description is closer to a larger ferry/catamaran: air-conditioned cabin, rows of seats, windows, snacks/café, and a large passenger load. Traveler reports on fast boats more generally describe a seated cabin, engine noise, TV/noise, strong AC or humidity depending on boat, basic toilets, stops along the river, and little ability to move around compared with a cruise.

Common comfort reports:

  • Bring warm layer: AC can be cold.
  • Bring snacks/water even if there is a café or vendors.
  • Choose seats away from toilets/engine if you can.
  • Download entertainment; do not count on usable internet.
  • Keep valuables and documents close.
  • Expect a long, repetitive ride: river views, stops, noise, and many hours in a seat.

Firsthand traveler sentiment

Overall sentiment: acceptable and useful, but not “fun” in the romantic Amazon-adventure sense. Most travelers who choose the fast boat seem glad they avoided the 2-3 day slow boat, but they still describe the ride as tiring, bureaucratically confusing, and schedule-uncertain.

Positive themes:

  • Memorable way to enter the Peruvian Amazon.
  • Much faster than the slow/cargo boat.
  • Feels like real Amazon travel rather than another flight.
  • Logistically possible with patience and local verification.
  • Larger ferry-style options feel safer/more comfortable than small informal boats.

Negative themes:

  • Online information is poor and often outdated.
  • Ticketing/confirmation can be stressful.
  • Border formalities are easy to mishandle.
  • Ride time can be longer than advertised.
  • Comfort issues: cold AC, non-reclining/limited seats, toilet smell/noise near the back, loud TV/music, humidity/musty cabin, little sleep.

Practical itinerary

  • Day 1: Fly Bogotá/Medellín -> Leticia. Sleep in Leticia.
  • Day 2: Confirm boat, get Colombia exit stamp, cross to Santa Rosa, enter Peru, board boat if timing works. If not, sleep Leticia/Santa Rosa/Tabatinga and leave next service day.
  • Day 3: Arrive Iquitos, depending on departure time and river conditions.

If booking onward flights, ayahuasca retreats, or tours from Iquitos, leave at least one buffer day after expected arrival.

What Leticia is really like

Leticia is a functional Amazon border town, not a polished resort town. Its appeal is access: the Colombian Amazon, the tri-border with Brazil and Peru, river tours, jungle lodges, Puerto Nariño, indigenous/community tourism options, and the novelty of crossing into Tabatinga, Brazil.

Good for 1-3 nights depending on whether you want Amazon activities. If only transiting, one night is enough; if you want Puerto Nariño/jungle/day tours, add more.

Verdict

Do it if you want a memorable overland/river-style Amazon transition and can tolerate uncertainty. It is a good travel experience in the “interesting and real” sense, not in the “comfortable and smooth” sense.

Best version: Bogotá -> Leticia flight, 1 night Leticia, CONFLUAM/Ferry Amazonas from Santa Rosa to Iquitos, with at least one buffer day before anything important in Iquitos.
