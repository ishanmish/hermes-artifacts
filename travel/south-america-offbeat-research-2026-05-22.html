A readable version of the suggested Colombia → Peru → Bolivia route, layered onto the earlier Hermes offbeat-research shortlist. The itinerary is preserved as a planning surface, not treated as a final booking plan.

Core route
Colombia → Peru → Bolivia

Nightlife and soft landing first, Amazon/spiritual middle, surreal high-altitude ending.

Must-not-cut
Medellín · Iquitos · Cusco · La Paz · Uyuni

Altitude + Bolivia logistics

Cusco/La Paz/Uyuni all need buffers. Bolivia overland legs should remain flexible.

1–3ColombiaCartagenaCaribbean entry, colour, nightlife, old-town energy.Old city, Getsemaní, beaches/islands, bars.Can cut to 2 days if schedule gets tight.
  • Pros/positives: travellers consistently like the walled city/Getsemaní colour, photogenic streets, warm Caribbean energy, rooftop/bar scene, and an easy first landing in Colombia.
  • Cons/negatives: many complain about aggressive vendors, touts, tourist pricing, scams, beach quality near town, and a feeling that the old city can become a polished tourist bubble.
  • Be aware: use Cartagena for the historic/nightlife opener, not for pristine beaches; pick Rosario/Barú day trips carefully, negotiate/confirm prices, and keep phones/cash discreet at night.
Medium-high Reddit signal
  • Next: Cartagena → Medellín.
  • Best: fly CTG → MDE; the bus is very long and rarely worth a 42-day itinerary slot.
  • Practical: land at José María Córdova and use airport bus/taxi/app transfer to El Poblado or Laureles.
4–8ColombiaMedellínBest nightlife anchor and modern Colombia base.El Poblado/Laureles, Comuna 13, Guatapé day trip, nightlife.Must-not-cut.
  • Pros/positives: strong praise for weather, nightlife, social hostel/digital-nomad energy, Comuna 13, Guatapé, restaurants/cafés, and an easy base in Poblado/Laureles.
  • Cons/negatives: Reddit is unusually caution-heavy on Medellín now: dating-app robberies, drink-spiking/scopolamine stories, phone theft, sex/drug-tourism risks, and Poblado feeling overrun by foreigners.
  • Be aware: still worth keeping, but treat it as high-reward/high-discipline: avoid Tinder/stranger apartments, never get sloppy drunk alone, use app transport late, and keep nightlife conservative.
High Reddit signal
  • Next: Medellín → Salento/Cocora.
  • Best: fly MDE → Pereira or Armenia, then taxi/bus/collectivo to Salento.
  • Budget fallback: bus via Pereira/Armenia, but it can eat most of a day on mountain roads.
9–11ColombiaSalento / Cocora ValleyNature break, coffee region, wax palms.Cocora Valley, coffee farms, town wandering.Good contrast after Medellín.
  • Pros/positives: travellers love Cocora Valley’s wax palms, coffee-farm visits, cooler mountain climate, and the slower small-town reset after Cartagena/Medellín.
  • Cons/negatives: common complaints are rain/mud, crowds on the classic Cocora loop, limited nightlife, cash/ATM friction, and transport connections being slower than they look.
  • Be aware: start Cocora early, bring rain layers and proper shoes, keep expectations rustic, and book onward transport with margin.
High Reddit signal
  • Next: Salento/Cocora → Bogotá.
  • Best: bus/taxi to Pereira or Armenia, then fly to Bogotá.
  • Budget fallback: direct/connecting bus to Bogotá, but allow 8–10+ hours and possible delays.
12–14ColombiaBogotáCapital, food, museums, grittier urban culture.La Candelaria, museums, Monserrate, nightlife.First cut candidate: 3 → 2 days.
  • Pros/positives: Reddit travellers value the museums, La Candelaria, Monserrate, food/café scene, urban culture, and the fact that it feels less polished than tourist Colombia.
  • Cons/negatives: negatives are cold/grey weather, traffic, altitude, sketchy blocks, phone theft, and some people finding it less immediately fun than Medellín or Cartagena.
  • Be aware: base in Chapinero/Zona G/Zona T rather than isolated Candelaria nights; do Monserrate in daylight; use ride apps and keep it to 2 days if energy is low.
Medium-high Reddit signal
  • Next: Bogotá → Lima.
  • Best: direct international flight BOG → LIM.
  • Practical: avoid tight self-connects; immigration/check-in can be slow, and Lima traffic matters after arrival.
15–17PeruLimaFood, coastal city, recovery between countries.Miraflores, Barranco, restaurants, coastline.Sets up Iquitos flight.
  • Pros/positives: very strong praise for food, ceviche, Barranco/Miraflores, ocean cliffs, good hotels, and easy logistics before the Amazon.
  • Cons/negatives: many travellers call Lima grey, traffic-heavy, sprawling, and not a beach city despite the coast; some feel it is less exciting outside food/neighbourhood wandering.
  • Be aware: use Lima deliberately for food and recovery, book key restaurants early, stay Miraflores/Barranco/San Isidro, and do not overbuild beach expectations.
High Reddit signal
  • Next: Lima → Iquitos.
  • Best/only practical: fly LIM → IQT; Iquitos has no road connection to the rest of Peru.
  • Practical: leave buffer if connecting to a retreat pickup or Amazon lodge boat.
18–24PeruIquitos / Amazon / AyahuascaDeep offbeat Amazon/spiritual block.Amazon lodge/river, Iquitos, ayahuasca retreat if chosen.Must-not-cut, but choose operator carefully.
  • Pros/positives: travellers describe the Amazon river/lodge experience as genuinely unique; good retreats can be profound, communal, and far more memorable than another city stop.
  • Cons/negatives: repeated concerns are fake/unsafe shamans, sexual/financial exploitation risks, medical contraindications, intense ceremonies, heat, bugs, muddy logistics, and Iquitos city being rough around the edges.
  • Be aware: vet retreat/operator harder than hotels: medical screening, no hard-sell, clear facilitator ratio, emergency plan, dieta rules, no mixing with SSRIs/contraindicated meds, and avoid picking only by lowest price.
Medium Reddit signal; operator-specific
  • Next: Iquitos → Lima.
  • Best/only practical: fly IQT → LIM.
  • Practical: keep Day 25 as a true buffer for laundry, food, sleep, and possible Amazon/flight delays.
25PeruLimaTransit / recovery day.Fly back from Iquitos, reset, laundry, rest.Important buffer before altitude.
  • Pros/positives: travellers usually appreciate a decompression day here because it prevents Amazon delays from breaking the Cusco/Machu Picchu chain.
  • Cons/negatives: it is not a destination day if you already did Lima; moving hotels/airports can feel like dead time.
  • Be aware: this buffer is sensible before altitude; do not schedule a hard Cusco arrival plus strenuous activity immediately after the Amazon.
Planning/logistics signal
  • Next: Lima → Cusco/Sacred Valley.
  • Best: morning flight LIM → CUZ, then a light first day in Cusco or transfer lower to Sacred Valley if altitude-sensitive.
  • Practical: avoid same-day hard tours; flights into Cusco can be weather-disrupted.
26–31PeruCusco / Sacred ValleyAltitude acclimatization, Inca sites, Machu Picchu setup.Cusco, San Blas, Sacsayhuamán, Sacred Valley, Ollantaytambo, Machu Picchu.First day in Cusco should be light.
  • Pros/positives: Reddit is overwhelmingly positive on Cusco, Sacred Valley, Ollantaytambo, and Machu Picchu as the iconic Peru block with ruins, hikes, markets, scenery, and great social energy.
  • Cons/negatives: altitude sickness, overtourism, early trains/buses, timed-entry ticket stress, and tour-sales fatigue are the recurring complaints.
  • Be aware: acclimatize first, book Machu Picchu/train/bus tickets early, use Ollantaytambo as a smart staging base, and keep the first 24–48 hours light.
High Reddit signal
  • Within block: Cusco/Sacred Valley → Machu Picchu → Cusco.
  • Best: Cusco/Urubamba/Pisac by taxi/collectivo; stage in Ollantaytambo; train to Aguas Calientes; bus/walk to Machu Picchu; train back to Ollanta/Cusco.
  • Practical: do not make the Machu day a same-day Lima/Cusco connection; ticket windows are unforgiving.
32PeruCuscoReturn to Cusco / nightlife.Cusco Plaza de Armas, San Blas, bars/restaurants.Use as decompression after Machu Picchu/Sacred Valley.
  • Pros/positives: travellers like Cusco as a post-Machu reset: walkable, social, good food/bars, pretty San Blas/Plaza streets, and easy tour logistics.
  • Cons/negatives: touristy prices, touts, party-hostel noise, and feeling over-commercialized compared with Sacred Valley towns.
  • Be aware: treat this as decompression plus optional nightlife, not a packed sightseeing day.
Medium-high Reddit signal
  • Next: stay in Cusco for Humantay/Rainbow day.
  • Best: book a reputable day-tour pickup from Cusco; private driver only if you want control and can handle the early start.
  • Practical: sleep early; most tours leave pre-dawn.
33PeruCuscoHumantay Lake or Rainbow Mountain / buffer.Humantay Lake preferred; Rainbow Mountain optional.Altitude is real. Keep this flexible.
  • Pros/positives: both Humantay and Rainbow get praise for dramatic high-Andes scenery and big visual payoff; Humantay is often viewed as the prettier/cleaner choice if picking one.
  • Cons/negatives: Reddit complaints focus on brutal altitude, very early starts, crowds, weather/clouds, horse traffic, and Rainbow sometimes feeling like an Instagram conveyor belt.
  • Be aware: choose one, not both, unless acclimatized and energetic; skip if sick; bring layers, water, snacks, and be honest about altitude symptoms.
Medium Reddit signal
  • Next: Cusco → La Paz.
  • Best: fly if a workable CUZ → LPB routing exists for your dates; otherwise fly via Lima rather than burning a full overland day.
  • Scenic/budget fallback: bus via Puno/Copacabana/Desaguadero, but expect a long border day and more altitude fatigue.
34–37BoliviaLa PazChaotic, high-altitude, weird, indigenous, markets, cable cars, dramatic geography.Mi Teleférico, Witches’ Market, Cholita wrestling, Valle de la Luna, Death Road biking, Sopocachi bars.Stay: Sopocachi. One of the most offbeat cities in South America.
  • Pros/positives: travellers call La Paz one of South America’s most unique cities: cable-car views, mountains, markets, Cholitas, weird topography, and a raw/offbeat feel.
  • Cons/negatives: altitude hits hard, traffic/chaos, rough edges, petty theft risk, cold nights, and Death Road safety depends heavily on operator quality.
  • Be aware: stay Sopocachi, keep arrival day light, use Mi Teleférico as transport/sightseeing, pick a reputable Death Road company, and do not underestimate 3,600m+ altitude.
High Reddit signal
  • Next: La Paz → Uyuni.
  • Best budget: overnight tourist bus (e.g., higher-comfort operators) to Uyuni, saving a hotel night.
  • Best comfort: fly La Paz → Uyuni if schedules and baggage work; still arrive with warm layers for the 3-day tour.
38–40BoliviaUyuni Salt FlatsRemote, surreal, desert, salt flats, lagoons, stars.3-day Salar tour, mirror flats if season supports it, train cemetery, Incahuasi Island, red/green lagoons, flamingos, stargazing.Non-negotiable Bolivia highlight.
  • Pros/positives: Reddit sentiment is very strong: Salar, islands, colored lagoons, flamingos, desert, stars, and the sheer alien scale are viewed as a trip highlight.
  • Cons/negatives: the 3-day jeep tour is cold, rough, dusty, basic, altitude-heavy, and operator quality varies; bad food/lodging and unsafe drivers are common warning themes.
  • Be aware: prioritize operator safety/reputation over price, bring layers/sunscreen/power bank/snacks, expect no luxury, and confirm whether the tour ends in Uyuni, San Pedro, or loops back.
High Reddit signal
  • Next: Uyuni → Sucre or Copacabana/Isla del Sol.
  • Recommended ending route: Uyuni → Potosí → Sucre by bus/private transfer; break in Potosí only if you want the historic mining city.
  • If choosing lake ending: Uyuni → La Paz → Copacabana by bus is much longer and less efficient than Sucre.
41–42BoliviaSucre or Copacabana / Isla del SolChoose city-culture ending or peaceful lake/nature ending.See option table below.Recommendation from pasted itinerary: choose Sucre.
  • Pros/positives: Sucre gets praise as pretty, relaxed, lower-altitude, café/student-town decompression after Uyuni; Copacabana/Isla del Sol gets praise for lake sunsets, slow pace, and a peaceful spiritual finish.
  • Cons/negatives: Sucre can feel quiet if you want a big finale; Copacabana can feel touristy/basic and adds more overland time; Isla logistics/weather can underwhelm if rushed.
  • Be aware: for this 42-day route, Sucre is the better default because it adds city culture and smoother onward flights via Santa Cruz/La Paz; choose the lake only if you specifically want a slow nature ending.
Medium Reddit signal
  • Next: Bolivia → NYC.
  • Best if Sucre: fly SRE → Santa Cruz or La Paz, then international connection home.
  • Best if Copacabana/Isla: bus back to La Paz, then fly home; keep a buffer before the international leg.

San Cipriano “brujitas” rail carts · Colombia
Jungle river reserve reached by informal wooden carts powered by motorbikes on rail tracks. Pair with river tubing and birding. Caveat: informal transport; go daytime and re-check current safety/logistics.
Salar de Uyuni + Eduardo Avaroa · Bolivia
3–4 day high-altitude jeep circuit through salt flats, lagoons, flamingos, geysers, deserts, volcanoes, and remote terrain. Main caveats: altitude, cold, operator quality, roads, and protest/roadblock disruptions.
Marcahuasi / Markawasi mystery plateau · Peru
High plateau near Lima with strange rock formations and archaeological-mystery vibe. Needs verification on access route, altitude/overnight needs, safety, season, and guided vs self-guided.
Cerros de Mavecure · Colombia
Remote granite hills in the Amazon-Orinoco region. Unusual and far from the mainstream Colombia itinerary. Needs access/permit/guide/safety/seasonality verification.
