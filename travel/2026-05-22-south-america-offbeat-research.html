South America offbeat shortlist: Peru, Bolivia, Colombia

TL;DR: Use Colombia for weird, logistically flexible experiences; use Bolivia only if the road/protest situation is clean; keep Peru as a near-Lima mystery/altitude candidate that needs logistics verification before booking.
Best weirdness / friction ratio
San Cipriano

Motorbike-powered rail carts through jungle, plus river reserve. Strong “only-in-Colombia” energy.

Most surreal anchor
Uyuni + Eduardo Avaroa

Not hidden, but the landscape is too alien to ignore if Bolivia logistics are stable.

Needs verification
Marcahuasi + Mavecure

High upside but requires checks on access, guides, seasonality, safety, and time needed.

!
Decision framing: this artifact is not a final itinerary. It is a ranked research surface: what to keep, what to verify, and what not to book until logistics are known.

Priority picks

San Cipriano “brujitas” rail carts Colombia · Valle del Cauca

What it is: a jungle river reserve reached by informal wooden carts powered by motorbikes on rail tracks.

  • Why it made the list: very Colombia-specific, memorable, and strange without needing a remote expedition.
  • Pair with: river tubing, reserve walk, birding.
  • Caveat: informal transport; go daytime and re-check current safety/logistics.
Salar de Uyuni + Eduardo Avaroa jeep circuit Bolivia · Southwest

What it is: a 3–4 day high-altitude jeep circuit through salt flats, lagoons, flamingos, geysers, deserts, volcanoes, and remote terrain.

  • Why it made the list: not undiscovered, but still probably the strongest surreal/offbeat anchor in Bolivia.
  • Main caveat: altitude, cold, operator quality, roads, and any protest/roadblock disruptions.
  • Booking posture: keep modular until route stability is confirmed.
Marcahuasi / Markawasi mystery plateau Peru · Lima region

What it is: a high plateau near Lima with strange rock formations and an archaeological-mystery vibe.

  • Why it made the list: good weird alternative to polished Peru tourism if you want something eerie near Lima.
  • Open questions: access route, altitude/overnight needs, safety, season, guided vs self-guided.
Cerros de Mavecure Colombia · Guainía

What it is: remote granite hills in the Amazon-Orinoco region, surfaced in offbeat Colombia research.

  • Why it made the list: unusual and far from the mainstream Colombia itinerary.
  • Open questions: access point, permits/guides, safety, seasonality, how many days needed.
