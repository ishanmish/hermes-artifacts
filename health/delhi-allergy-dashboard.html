Delhi allergy dashboard

A practical, reusable plan for sneezing + itchy nose after coming to Delhi — likely allergic rhinitis triggered by dust, pollution, pollen, AC/dry air, or indoor allergens.

Bottom line: yes, an air purifier is useful — especially in the bedroom — but the highest-yield combo is air cleanup + saline rinse + non-drowsy antihistamine when flaring. If symptoms persist, a nasal steroid spray is usually more effective than repeated antihistamines. See a doctor if red flags show up.

Immediate action plan

Today
Run purifier in bedroom. High mode for 30–60 minutes, then auto/medium. Keep windows mostly closed. If no purifier yet, use N95 outdoors and wash face/shower after traffic/dust exposure.
Nasal hygiene
Saline spray or rinse. 2–4×/day spray, or one rinse/day. Use sterile/distilled/boiled-then-cooled water for rinses.
Symptom relief
Non-drowsy antihistamine if flaring. Fexofenadine is usually least sedating. Cetirizine/levocetirizine may work well but can make you sleepy.
If persistent
Nasal steroid spray. Fluticasone or mometasone daily for 1–2 weeks is often the most effective allergy control. Aim slightly outward, not at the septum.
